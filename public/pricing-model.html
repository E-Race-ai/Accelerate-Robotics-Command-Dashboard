What to Tell Brent

“We’re pricing this at $10.50 per hour, per bot. That’s all-inclusive — the robot, deployment, training, maintenance, consumables, monitoring, everything. You pay one number per hour of operation.

Your loaded labor cost for housekeeping is $22–$28 per hour. This is half that, works 12+ hours a day, 7 days a week, and never calls in sick.

For the pilot, we’ll do $8.50 per hour for 90 days. Two bots — one for carpet, one for hard floors. If it works, we add delivery and transport. If it doesn’t, you walk away with zero commitment.

We’re not asking you to buy robots. We’re asking you to replace a line item in your labor budget with a cheaper, more reliable, always-available alternative.”

What Not to Say Tomorrow

  • Don’t mention Atlas Mobility, healthcare, or the pivot — this is Accelerate Robotics
  • Don’t promise elevator integration in Phase 1 — cleaning bots don’t need elevators, save that for Phase 2/3
  • Don’t commit to all 4 bot types immediately — start with 2, prove it, expand
  • Don’t quote monthly or annual numbers unless asked — hourly is psychologically easier to absorb

Key Assumptions to Validate

  1. Utilization rate (75%) — track actual charging time, downtime, and maintenance during pilot
  2. Consumable costs ($135/mo) — actuals may vary by floor type, traffic, and cleaning frequency
  3. Repair reserve (10%/year) — adjust up or down based on actual incident rate
  4. Management labor ($625/bot/mo) — will decrease per-bot as fleet scales; increase if on-site presence required
  5. 12 billable hours/day — validate battery life and charging cycles in real conditions

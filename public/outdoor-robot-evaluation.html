Accelerate Robotics — Outdoor Cleaning Robot Evaluation

THesis Hotel Miami (Curio Collection by Hilton) — Paseo Courtyard — Comprehensive Market Scan, Q2 2026

Working Draft Confidential Working Draft

The Thesis Hotel has a palm-lined ground-level Paseo Courtyard between the hotel and residence towers that needs a dedicated cleaning robot to handle tropical debris: palm fronds, leaves, flower litter, and light trash. The courtyard also houses a pop-up padel court ("Privé Padel at THesis") and is programmed as an "Urban Living Room" for yoga, farmers markets, weddings, and live music. This document is a full market scan of every robot that could plausibly do the job — commercial outdoor sweepers, semi-outdoor sweepers, modular yard robots, industrial street sweepers, wet scrubbers, and consumer patio vacuums — with a fit-scored recommendation at the end.

Every robot in this evaluation was verified against manufacturer spec sheets and current 2026 availability. Pricing is indicative only; quote every candidate before committing.

1. The Use Case

The Space

The Paseo Courtyard is a long, narrow ground-level space between the 10-story hotel wing and the 6-story residence tower of the Paseo de la Riviera mixed-use project, designed by Jorge Hernandez Architects with landscape by Geomantic Designs. It is marketed as an "Urban Living Room" — semi-private, open to residents, UM students, neighbors, and hotel guests. Any robot that operates there is visible to paying guests every hour of the day, so aesthetics, noise, and safety posture matter as much as cleaning performance.

Dimensions: 25 ft × 202 ft (confirmed)
Footprint: ~5,050 sq ft cocktail area (one source says ~8,232 sq ft — conflict flagged)
Surface: Palm-lined hardscape, exact material unconfirmed (pavers most likely)
Cover: Open to sky — padel and outdoor yoga confirm this
Landscape: Towering palms and colorful blooms (Geomantic Designs, Mediterranean-tropical mix)
Sport court: Privé Padel pop-up (~65 × 33 ft, ~32% of courtyard length)
Access path: Hotel lobby and residence tower; ground-level
Pedestrian traffic: Continuous; residents, UM students, neighbors, guests
Site-fact correction from Q2 2026 profile: Earlier drafts assumed an 800–1,500 m² (~8,600–16,100 sq ft) courtyard. The confirmed footprint is a 25 ft × 202 ft linear corridor, much narrower and more constrained than originally scoped. Additionally, the sport court is padel, not pickleball or tennis — Privé Padel at THesis is reportedly the 1,000th padel court in the USA and the first in Coral Gables. The court takes up roughly a third of the courtyard length when deployed. See thesis-hotel-site-profile.html for full context.

Programming (Why the Courtyard Is Rarely Empty)

Unlike a typical hotel courtyard that sits quiet between events, the Paseo is the project's "Urban Living Room" and carries dense programming throughout the week:

  • Outdoor yoga: Thursdays 9:30 AM and Saturdays 10 AM (Ahana Yoga, "Gentle Warrior Saturdays")
  • Padel play: pop-up court, hours unpublished (expect daytime + early evening)
  • Live music, farmers markets, Herpreneur Artisan Market — recurring community programming
  • Weddings & events: up to 500 cocktail / 200 banquet — the hotel's largest single venue
  • Walk-through traffic: residents, UM students, and neighbors routinely traverse the corridor between wings
Operational window is narrow. Combined with the rooftop pool / bar hours (6:30pm pool / 11pm Ivy kitchen) and the hotel's L1 restaurant hours, the inferred autonomous window for outdoor courtyard cleaning is ~11pm–6am daily, with tighter constraints on nights when the courtyard is hosting a wedding or market.

The Debris

Florida tropical landscaping generates a specific type of litter that is unusual compared to an airport or a warehouse:

  • Palm fronds (up to 2 ft, stringy and fibrous — will choke a vacuum, needs a brush to be swept)
  • Broad tropical leaves (Monstera, Ficus, broad-leaf bougainvillea — moist, sticky in humidity)
  • Flower petals and seed pods from bougainvillea, plumeria, hibiscus
  • Light guest trash — cocktail napkins, straws, wrappers, pool towels
  • Fine dust — salt aerosol, windblown sand (Miami coastal)
  • Wet/dry mixed after afternoon rain or sprinkler cycles

The Climate

Summer high: 95°F / 35°C
Humidity: 75–95% year-round
Rain: Daily afternoon showers, June–Oct
Sun exposure: Direct UV, 8+ hours most days
Salt air: Coastal corrosion risk
Wind: Gulf storms, hurricane season

No-Go Zones

  • Padel court interior when a match is in play — dynamic obstacle, ball chases, spectators, equipment
  • Padel court glass walls / structure — even off-hours, the glass enclosure is a fragile obstacle; no contact
  • Yoga class footprint on Thursday 9:30 AM and Saturday 10 AM — robot must not be operating within the courtyard during these windows
  • Planter beds and palm root flares (robot must not pull mulch or damage drip irrigation)
  • Event setup / teardown during weddings, markets, or live music programming
  • Guest-seating areas when occupied (The Collab / Mamey outdoor seating may spill into the Paseo)
  • Stairs / step-down thresholds at the hotel and residence tower entrances (any robot without step detection is disqualified)

2. Evaluation Criteria

Every candidate is scored against eight criteria. The first four are hard gates — a robot must pass all four to be considered. The next four are preference factors.

Hard Gates (Must-Pass)

GateRequirement
Surface compatibilityMust operate on hard flat tile/stone. Robots designed for grass or dirt only are out.
Weather ratingMinimum IP54 (dust + rain splash). Ideally IP55 or higher. Must tolerate Florida humidity without condensation damage.
Debris handlingMust pick up leaves and light debris, not just fine dust. Rules out residential robot vacuums and most floor scrubbers.
Guest-area safetyIndependent safety certification OR proven deployment in guest-facing hospitality environments. Must have visual/audio warning system for pedestrian awareness.

4. Candidate Deep-Dives

Fourteen robots were evaluated individually. Each was graded Top Pick, Strong Candidate, Weak Fit, or Rejected based on the criteria in Section 2.

1. PUDU MT1 Max Top Pick

Category A — Commercial Outdoor Sweeper · Manufacturer: Pudu Robotics

The MT1 Max is the newest flagship in Pudu's sweeper line, launched specifically to address semi-outdoor environments like courtyards, apartment complexes, and parking structures. It is the only robot in this evaluation that is purpose-built for exactly this use case.

IP rating: IP54 (dust + rain splash)
Rain sensor: Yes — auto-returns to safe zone
Sweep width: 27.5 in (70 cm)
Waste bin: 9.2 gal (35 L)
Cleaning rate: 1,800–6,000 m²/hr
Runtime: 4–8 hrs, auto-dock
Brushes: Dual-disc + main roller
Sensing: 3D LiDAR + AI cameras, 150 m detection
Safety: 1.2 m warning light + audible alarms
Indicative price: ~$24,000 US (list)

Why it fits

  • IP54 + rain sensor handles Florida showers
  • Dual-disc brushes actually pick up palm fronds and leaves (not just vacuum fines)
  • 35 L bin = half-day of tropical debris without emptying
  • AI debris recognition continuously learns new litter types
  • 3D LiDAR navigates glass rails, sculptures, and planters safely
  • Hospitality-grade design — looks intentional, not industrial
  • Auto-dock and 24/7 operation — runs three cycles per day unattended

Watch-outs

  • IP54 ≠ waterproof — direct heavy rain will still dock the robot
  • US distributor density is lighter than Europe; lead time TBD
  • 1.2 m warning light is tall — confirm it doesn't clash with courtyard sightlines
  • ~$24K capex; must be wrapped into RaaS or financed
  • Padel court as dynamic obstacle — 3D LiDAR must reliably detect and avoid glass enclosure walls; to confirm in loaner week
  • 25 ft wide courtyard needs ~11 sweep passes across the width with a 70 cm brush; confirm pattern-planner can handle the long linear geometry

2. PUDU MT1 (Base Model) Strong Candidate

The original MT1 on which the Max is based. Same AI platform, same dual-disc brush design, same 35 L bin, but without the IP54 rating, without the 3D LiDAR upgrade, and without the water-drop sensor. Best suited for covered or indoor areas.

IP rating: None rated (indoor/covered only)
Sweep width: 27.5 in (70 cm)
Waste bin: 9.2 gal (35 L)
Cleaning rate: 1,800 m²/hr standard
Runtime: 4–8 hrs, auto-dock
Sensing: LiDAR SLAM + VSLAM
Target area: Up to 100,000 m²
Indicative price: ~$17–$19K US (~30% less than Max)

Why it fits

Watch-outs

Primary: PUDU MT1 Max

The MT1 Max is the only robot in the fourteen-candidate scan that was purpose-built for this exact environment. IP54 weather sealing, dual-disc brushes that physically sweep palm fronds and leaves, 35 L bin for tropical debris volume, 3D LiDAR for the obstacle-dense courtyard, rain-drop sensor for the Florida afternoon showers, and hospitality-grade visual aesthetics.

Every other commercial option either (a) is indoor-only and will fail on the first rainy day, or (b) is a scrubber that's wrong for dry leaves, or (c) is a consumer vacuum that will die on the first palm frond. The MT1 Max is the only machine that clears all four hard gates.

Runner-up: PUDU MT1 (if courtyard is fully covered)

If site inspection reveals that the courtyard sweep path is 100% under a glass roof, atrium structure, or loggia — the base MT1 is a ~30% cheaper path to the same brush and bin hardware. Confirm first that no robot path is exposed to direct rain or sprinkler overspray.

Experimental backup: Yarbo leaf-blower module

Only if the cleaning window allows staff to follow behind and pick up blower piles. Not a complete solution — but a cheap complement to handle heavy post-storm debris that would overwhelm a sweeper's bin.

7. Pilot Path

This is the recommended 4-week sequence to evaluate and deploy the MT1 Max at the Thesis Hotel courtyard. It mirrors the indoor-fleet playbook that is already running at the same property, so the hotel's GM sees a consistent process.

Week 1 — Site Survey

  • On-site walk with a PUDU US distributor representative
  • Confirm courtyard dimensions, door widths, elevator cab clearances
  • Map the sweep path, no-go zones, and docking station location
  • Measure typical debris load (weight / day) to validate the 35 L bin assumption
  • Confirm Wi-Fi coverage and charging power availability

Week 2 — Loaner Evaluation

  • 7-day on-site loaner from PUDU distributor
  • Run both a morning and evening cleaning cycle
  • Stress test: trigger rain event, measure response time of water-drop sensor
  • Collect guest feedback via GM + front-desk log
  • Document edge cases (fountain splash, planter overshoot, stair avoidance)

Week 3 — Commercial Proposal

  • Present RaaS pricing to Thesis Hotel GM (~$2,850/mo flat, bundled with indoor fleet)
  • Draft Phase 4 addendum to the existing proposal document
  • Confirm maintenance SLA and spare-parts access

Week 4 — Commissioning

  • Ship the production unit
  • 3-day on-site setup, mapping, and staff training (same playbook as indoor fleet)
  • Go-live + 30-day monitoring window

8. Deployment Risks Specific to Outdoor Use

Risk Likelihood Impact Mitigation
Heavy rain event exceeds IP54 protection High Medium Water-drop sensor returns robot to covered dock. Verify dock is under a canopy/loggia during site survey.
Salt-air corrosion of chassis over time Medium Medium Include salt-wipe protocol in weekly maintenance. Negotiate salt corrosion into warranty terms with distributor.
Direct UV exposure damages cameras / LiDAR housing Low Medium Position dock in shaded area; schedule cleaning cycles to avoid peak sun (late morning / early evening).
Robot falls down step / ledge not detected Low High During commissioning, explicitly map all drop-offs as no-go zones. Test 3D LiDAR step detection in real courtyard conditions.
Guest collision or startle event Medium High Rely on 1.2 m warning light + audible alerts. Schedule most aggressive cleaning in off-peak hours (5–7 AM).
Large debris (storm/hurricane aftermath) overwhelms robot Medium Medium Manual pre-sweep by facilities staff after any severe weather. Robot handles the detail, humans handle the bulk.
Humidity-driven condensation damages electronics Medium High Climate-controlled dock enclosure. Run full dry-down cycle before docking in wet conditions.
RaaS margin compression from consumables in salt air Medium Low Build in 15% maintenance premium for outdoor units in the financial model. Track actual vs. planned consumables for 6 months.

9. Conclusion

Fourteen robots looked at; exactly one was designed for this job. The PUDU MT1 Max is the only commercial-grade, hospitality-aesthetic, weather-rated, leaf-capable, fleet-managed sweeper that exists in the current market for an outdoor hotel courtyard in Florida. Everything else is either indoor, residential, wrong-category, or wrong-scale.

Recommend moving directly to a site survey and loaner evaluation with PUDU. Reassess only if (a) site survey reveals full-coverage roof, in which case the base MT1 becomes viable, or (b) PUDU US distribution lead time is unacceptable, in which case the fallback is to bundle Yarbo's leaf-blower module with a human follow-up crew as a temporary bridge.

A Phase 4 addendum to the Thesis Hotel proposal is the cleanest commercial structure: one additional RaaS line item, ~$2,850/mo flat, integrated with the existing indoor-fleet service contract.

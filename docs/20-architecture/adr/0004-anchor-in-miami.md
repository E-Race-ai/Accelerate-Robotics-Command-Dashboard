# ADR 0004 — Anchor the company in Miami

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-01-05
- **Deciders:** Eric

## Context

Accelerate Robotics needs a home base. The default assumption in robotics and AI startups is San Francisco or Boston — that's where the talent density, VC concentration, and adjacent companies are. But two considerations push against that default for us:

1. **Our first customer is in Miami.** Thesis Hotel is our wedge deployment. We need to be physically close for install days, site surveys, and relationship management.
2. **Hospitality + healthcare operators in the Southeast are underserved.** Most robotics companies are chasing labs in SF or Silicon Valley enterprise pilots. We can own the Southeast by showing up.

The forces:

- Where do we recruit? Miami has fewer robotics engineers than SF, but more hospitality and healthcare operations expertise
- Where do we raise? Miami's VC scene is smaller but growing; remote VC is now normal
- Where do we demo? Close to Thesis Hotel = Miami; close to the next 10 customers = also Miami (hotels + hospitals in the region)
- Cost of living, tax environment, quality of life for the founder

## Options considered

### Option A: Miami

- **Pros:** Near first customer, underserved region, lower cost than SF, tax-advantageous, proximity to Latin American expansion
- **Cons:** Thinner engineering talent pool, less robotics peer network, harder to hire senior ML engineers

### Option B: San Francisco

- **Pros:** Talent, capital, peer network, proximity to every robotics and AI company
- **Cons:** Expensive, cultural mismatch with hospitality and healthcare operators, far from every current customer

### Option C: Boston

- **Pros:** Strong robotics legacy (iRobot, Boston Dynamics), tech talent, proximity to hospitals
- **Cons:** Same downsides as SF at slightly lower cost; far from warm-weather hospitality

### Option D: Fully remote, no office

- **Pros:** No anchor, lowest cost
- **Cons:** Hardware startups are physical — we need space for robots, boards, staging

## Decision

**Anchor in Miami.** Remote-friendly engineering is fine; physical ops and customer-facing roles are Miami-based. Customers and investors fly to us — we're close enough to the East Coast for day trips and a hub airport.

## Consequences

- **Positive:**
  - Same-day site visits to Thesis Hotel and every other hotel in the region
  - Tax treatment is better than California
  - Latin America expansion is natural
  - Differentiation from every SF robotics startup
- **Negative:**
  - Harder to hire senior robotics + ML engineers locally
  - Smaller peer network for technical founders
  - We're defending an unconventional choice every time we pitch an SF investor
- **Neutral:**
  - Remote-first for engineering — many roles don't need to be in Miami

## Follow-ups

- Build a local ecosystem (meetups, university partnerships — UM, FIU)
- Explore co-location with a hotel partner for early-stage demos
- Evaluate annual — do we still want to be here in 12 months?

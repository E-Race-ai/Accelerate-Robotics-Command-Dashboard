## 4. Residences (NOT Condos)

The residential tower, "The Residences at THesis," is a **rental apartment building**, not a branded-residence or condo-hotel. There is **no for-sale ownership** component.

- **204 luxury rental units**, studios through 3-bedroom
- Separate address: 1340 S Dixie Hwy
- Operator: **Gables Residential**
- Rents (2026): ~$3,329–$7,006/month
- 6 stories, ~519,000 sq ft
- Marketing references a separate "elevated pool deck" for residents — may be distinct from the hotel rooftop pool (unconfirmed)

**Implication for robot deployment:** The residence tower is a separate operational domain. Hotel-side robots should not need to cross into the residence wing. Confirm whether the rooftop pool is shared or only hotel-side.

## 5. Floor Plans & Layout

No publicly downloadable architectural floor plans surfaced. TLC Engineering's project page is the most detailed public engineering reference. Neither Gensler nor Jorge Hernandez Architects has posted a public project page.

**Two-building layout:**
- Hotel (10 stories) and residence tower (6 stories) flank a shared **ground-level Paseo Courtyard**
- Paseo is ~25 ft × 202 ft between the two wings

**Vertical organization (hotel):**
| Floor | Use |
|---|---|
| **L1** | Lobby, Mamey (restaurant), meeting/event space |
| **L2** | Meeting rooms, Theory Ballroom |
| **L3** | **Rooftop amenity deck — pool + Mamey on 3rd / Ivy Rooftop** |
| **L4–10** | Guest rooms |

Rooftop pool and rooftop bar are **co-located on floor 3**, not on a separate upper roof. This is the single most important correction to early assumptions about the site.

**Circulation:**
- Rooftop amenity deck accessed via hotel lobby elevators to L3
- Paseo Courtyard has entries from both the hotel lobby and the residence tower — semi-private, controlled but not guest-exclusive
- Dedicated pool-service elevator vs. shared guest elevators: not confirmed publicly

### 6b. Padel Court — CONFIRMED

The sport court in the Paseo Courtyard is **padel**, not pickleball and not tennis.

- **Brand:** "Privé Padel at THesis Hotel"
- **First padel court in Coral Gables**
- Reportedly the **1,000th padel court built in the USA** (per The Padel Paper / Tennis Tonic milestone coverage)
- Described as a **"pop-up" / exclusive court** installed on the Paseo courtyard
- Positioning: "athletes and socialites"
- **Unconfirmed:** exact dimensions (standard padel is 20×10 m / ~65×33 ft), surface (artificial turf expected), reservation fees, lesson/pro availability

**Implication for robot deployment:** Padel court is a significant dynamic obstacle in the 25×202 ft courtyard. A standard court (~65×33 ft) occupies ~32% of the courtyard length. Robot routing must handle players, ball chases, and court-side spectators.

## 11. Key Observations for Robot Deployment

This is the section to flag when planning any Accelerate Robotics work at the site.

- **Rooftop pool is on L3, not ground level.** The pool-deck slip-hazard use case now runs on a 3rd-floor rooftop surface reached via hotel lobby elevators. Deck material is unconfirmed (Kool-Deck vs. travertine vs. pavers all possible). Any pool-deck robot must fit in a guest elevator for initial placement.
- **Paseo Courtyard is long and narrow (25 × 202 ft).** Earlier site estimates of ~800–1,500 m² (8,600–16,100 sq ft) were too generous. The working figure is ~5,050 sq ft cocktail area with a ~65×33 ft padel court occupying roughly a third of the length.
- **Padel is confirmed in the courtyard** (not pickleball, not tennis). Dynamic obstacle during play. Robot routing must route around the court footprint or execute only during non-play windows.
- **Courtyard is semi-public.** The "Urban Living Room" framing means robots will mix with residents, UM students, neighbors, yoga classes (Thu/Sat mornings), farmers markets, padel players, wedding setups, and live-music events. This is the opposite of a controlled environment.
- **10-story hotel wing** with guest rooms on floors 4–10. Any room-service or delivery robot must traverse the full vertical stack via ThyssenKrupp TAC32T elevators (see `phase-2-elevator.md`).
- **Quiet-window inference:** Pool closes 6:30pm; Ivy Rooftop kitchen closes 11pm; Collab/Mamey dinner ends 10–11pm; Mamey breakfast starts 6:30am; pool opens 7am. **Likely autonomous window: ~11pm–6am.**
- **Event surges:** Paseo hosts up to 500-person cocktails and 200-seat dinners; Theory Ballroom up to 250 reception indoor. Robot routing should adapt to setup/breakdown schedules (expect load-in/load-out traffic in service corridors).
- **Back-of-house / loading dock:** not documented publicly. Must be confirmed on site walk.
- **Curio Collection under Hilton:** participates in LightStay/EarthView. Brand-level integrations (loyalty, Honors app, centralized ops standards) may bias against standalone robot tech unless Hilton-approved or explicitly carved out.
- **Meeting room AV standard** (Meeting Owl Pro, built-in share-screen in every room) signals a tech-friendly operations culture.
- **Destination fee of $30.51/night** already bundles wellness perks — a precedent for bundling "robot-enabled service" into a single guest line item (useful framing for RaaS commercial structure).
- **Two-building mixed-use** means hotel robots should stay on the hotel side. Residences are a separate Gables Residential property.

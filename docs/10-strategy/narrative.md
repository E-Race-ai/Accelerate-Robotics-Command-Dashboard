# Narrative — Support, Not Replacement

## The line we hold

**"Robots are not replacing healthcare workers. Robots are reducing their injuries, handling their most repetitive tasks, and expanding their capacity to focus on patient care."**

This isn't spin. It's the strategically correct position because:

1. **Healthcare workers are our users**, not our adversaries. They are the ones who will champion or block adoption on every unit floor.
2. **Hospitals can't replace nurses even if they wanted to.** The labor shortage is structural. The correct framing is capacity expansion, not headcount reduction.
3. **Union and regulatory headwinds against "replacement" framing are ferocious**, and rightly so. Any vendor positioning robots as labor replacement loses the long game.
4. **The actual ROI math works without any layoffs.** Injury avoidance, travel-nurse reduction, clinical time returned to bedside — these are all additive to headcount retention.

## What we say

- "Robots handle the repetitive, injury-prone work so nurses can spend more time with patients."
- "Hospital workers shouldn't be lifting 2,000 pounds of linens a day. Robots can do that. Nurses can do the work only humans can do."
- "This is workforce multiplication, not workforce replacement."
- "In a labor market where hospitals are paying $300/hour for travel nurses, giving your existing staff more capacity is the highest-leverage move you can make."

## What we never say

- "Automate away the workforce."
- "Replace your nurses with robots."
- "Cut labor costs."
- "24/7 productivity without breaks."

## What we emphasize

- **Worker safety and injury reduction** (huge moral and financial weight)
- **Dignity of the work** — nurses chose nursing to care for patients, not to push linen carts
- **Compliance and quality** — pressure injuries, falls, HAPI rates
- **Retention** — hospitals that invest in worker-support tech retain staff better

## Who we cite

- **Labor statistics** — BLS data on healthcare worker injury rates
- **Travel nurse economics** — $24B+ spent in 2022 alone
- **HAPI costs** — $26.8B annual, 95% preventable
- **Clinical voices** — nursing KOLs, SPHM advocates, patient safety researchers
- **Worker advocacy groups** — where their goals align with ours

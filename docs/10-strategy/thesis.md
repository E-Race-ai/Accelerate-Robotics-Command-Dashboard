# Strategic Thesis

## One brain, many bots

The long-term value in healthcare robotics sits in **the software layer that coordinates fleets of robots across vendors**, not in manufacturing individual robots.

Hospitals will have dozens of robot types from different manufacturers — cleaning, linens, meals, pharmacy, supplies, patient transport, sensing, eventually patient-assistive humanoids. Left alone, that becomes a zoo of incompatible vendor silos. The winner is whoever provides the orchestration platform that makes them act as one coordinated workforce.

## Problem

1. **Workforce shortages** — persistent labor gaps in nursing, environmental services, logistics, support. U.S. hospitals spent over $24B on travel nurses alone in 2022.
2. **Worker injuries** — healthcare workers, especially nurses, have one of the highest occupational injury rates in the country. Most of it is lifting, transferring, and repetitive movement.
3. **Operational inefficiency** — manual logistics (linens, meals, supplies, pharmacy, equipment) consume clinical time that should be spent at the bedside.
4. **Patient safety** — HAPIs, falls, and immobility complications affect millions annually. Tens of thousands of deaths. Most of it is preventable.

## The strategic insight

Robotics will become infrastructure inside hospitals, the same way automation became infrastructure in warehouses. The opportunity is not "build the best cleaning robot." The opportunity is to be **the platform that coordinates every robot in the building**.

A hospital signing up for Accelerate Robotics gets:

- One portal to manage every robot, regardless of manufacturer
- One set of workflows that route tasks to whichever robot can do the job
- One integration with the facility (elevators, EMR, facilities management, safety)
- One source of analytics, compliance, and ROI measurement

The individual robot becomes a commodity. The orchestration layer is the moat.

## Platform functions

- Fleet management (status, location, health of every robot)
- Task orchestration (dispatch, priority, load balancing)
- System health and predictive maintenance
- Compliance and safety auditing (ASME, HIPAA adjacencies)
- Hospital system integration (EMR, facilities, elevators, access control)
- Analytics and ROI measurement
- Multi-tenant fleet views (for healthcare systems running 10+ hospitals)

## Robot categories we orchestrate

- Logistics (linens, supplies)
- Meal delivery
- Environmental cleaning (C30 at Thesis Hotel is our first wedge)
- Pharmacy transport
- Patient transport
- Sensing and monitoring (Atlas Mobility turn tracking fits here)
- Future: patient-touching humanoids (Phase 3 — higher regulatory burden)

## Why now

- **Workforce gap is structural.** Not a post-pandemic blip. Hospitals will still be short-staffed in 2030.
- **Hardware is ready.** Keenon, Pudu, Bear Robotics, Diligent, Aethon — dozens of capable robots are shipping today.
- **Platform funding is flowing.** Bedrock Robotics raised $270M Series B (Capital G, NVIDIA Ventures) for *construction* fleet coordination — exact analog for healthcare.
- **Elevator integration is unsolved.** Every vendor solves it one-off. A cheap, universal solution is the wedge that gets us into buildings.
- **Atlas Mobility is already in hospitals.** We have a distribution channel that most robotics startups don't.

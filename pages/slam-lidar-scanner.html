01 · Concept

The form factor — why a wheel on a stick

Long featureless corridors are the worst case for handheld SLAM. A wheel encoder pins down scale and heading so the solver can't drift.

Hold it like a cane. Walk the building.

The pole rides at an angle. The wheel rolls along the floor, the LiDAR scans walls and ceiling, and a tick-per-rotation signal from the wheel encoder becomes a third sensor fused into SLAM alongside the LiDAR and IMU.

Why it works: parallel walls, repeating doors, matching carpets — the solver has nothing unique to lock onto. A wheel encoder gives a true distance measurement per tick, which pins scale down hard. Same trick NavVis uses in their trolley; nobody has put it on a stick.

LiDAR + IMU only

drift ≈ 2–4% of path

In a 100 m hotel corridor, the scan ends 2–4 m off. Map won't close on loops. Robots won't trust it.

LiDAR + IMU + wheel encoder

drift < 0.3%

Wheel odometry provides a direct distance measurement every tick, so the Kalman filter can't drift in scale. Loops close.

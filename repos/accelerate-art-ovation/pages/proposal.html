Room Service Choreography — Tzeva × W3

The W3 is a transport platform — it carries the tray between two humans. The choreography is designed so Chef Lumpkin’s Tzeva plating standards, Marriott Bonvoy service expectations, and health-code compliance are never compromised by the robot.

1

Guest orders. 10:47 PM via Marriott Bonvoy app, in-room tablet, or phone → Tzeva POS (Simphony / Toast).

2

Tzeva plates & loads. Runner plates on porcelain at the pass, then loads the tray into the W3’s enclosed compartment at the kitchen door. Taps “dispatch.”

3

W3 rides the service elevator. Dedicated BOH car when available; yields to any guest call. 4–6 min pass → door.

4

Guest notified. Bonvoy push + gentle knock-tone. W3 announces itself. Guest opens the door; top hatch unlocks on touch-auth.

5

Tray retrieved. Guest pulls the tray. W3 closes and returns to standby. Scheduled pickup sweep every 45 min for dishes.

What This Preserves

  • ✓ Tzeva plates, loads, retrieves. The robot replaces the elevator-and-corridor walk — the lowest-value leg of the trip, the one that used to need a late-night runner.
  • ✓ Food-safety compliance. Enclosed, insulated compartment; temperature logged per order; NSF-certified interior surfaces.
  • ✓ Autograph-standard presentation. The coral-and-gold wrap reads as hotel equipment, not vendor equipment. Guest opens the door and sees Art Ovation, not a Keenon logo.

What This Does NOT Claim

  • ✕ Not a replacement for in-room dining service on premium orders (tasting menus, wine service, table-side presentation). Humans still run those.
  • ✕ Not a carrier for items requiring active heating — no ovens on board. Insulated only.
  • ✕ Not a substitute for Tzeva runners during peak dinner service. Humans handle complex orders; W3 handles simple parallel runs (post-9 PM, breakfast, amenity drops).

Service-Elevator Strategy

Room-service runs (W3) use the BOH service elevator by default, with guest-yield protocol on the guest cars as a fallback. Cleaning bots (C40) operate 2–5 AM when car demand is minimum. Zero robot ever blocks a guest waiting for a lift.

How It Works

1 · Tag Attached

An asset tag is affixed to a linen tote, bar cart, or event-turnover kit. Each tag broadcasts a unique ID every few seconds.

2 · Anchors Listen

Ceiling + wall anchors pick up the signal. Three or more anchors triangulate the tag’s position in real time.

3 · Dashboard Updates

Position data streams to Todd’s dashboard. Staff see every tagged asset on a live floor map — where it is, how long it’s been idle.

4 · Robot Dispatched

Rules trigger action — linen cart idle >20 min in a corridor? The nearest robot is dispatched to retrieve it. No ticket, no radio call.

Same infrastructure; different use cases. Once anchors are live, new workflows are software configuration — not new hardware.

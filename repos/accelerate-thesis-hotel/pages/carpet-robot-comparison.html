Executive Summary

Context: Thesis Hotel Miami — 10-story, 245 keys, carpeted corridors floors 4-10

After researching 10+ manufacturers and their autonomous commercial cleaning robots, there are three strong alternatives to the Keenon C30 for the Thesis Hotel deployment, plus one emerging contender worth monitoring. The evaluation weighed carpet performance, elevator integration, noise level, fleet consolidation, US support availability, and price.

Robot Profiles

Detailed pros, cons, and verdict for each viable candidate

1

PUDU CC1 / CC1 Pro

PUDU Robotics · Shenzhen, China · 4-in-1 Commercial Cleaner

Primary Recommendation
17,000 Pa suction 700-1,000 sqm/hr 4 hr battery <70 dB Elevator integrated Single-vendor w/ MT1 Max $22K / $479-917mo

Pros

  • Strategic: PUDU already in Thesis fleet — single vendor, single platform, single relationship
  • 17,000 Pa suction in dedicated carpet vacuum mode
  • Highest coverage rate in comparison (700-1,000 sqm/hr)
  • Confirmed elevator integration via Elevator Control Module (no elevator modification)
  • CC1 Pro: rear AI camera for quality monitoring and auto surface detection
  • 4-in-1 versatility — carpet corridors AND hard-floor lobbies
  • Competitive pricing vs. dedicated carpet vacuums

Cons

  • Heavy at 75 kg
  • Carpet vacuum is one of four modes (dedicated nozzle sold separately)
  • <70 dB is highest among top contenders (still acceptable)
  • Vacuum-mode cleaning width narrower than scrub path
  • Newer to US market than SoftBank Whiz
Verdict: Strongest strategic recommendation. Consolidating on PUDU simplifies vendor management, support, and fleet software. One robot handles both carpet corridors and hard-floor lobbies. Elevator integration module is proven.
2

Gausium Vacuum 40

Gausium · Shanghai, China · Purpose-Built Commercial Vacuum

Strongest Performance
24 kPa (highest) 400-800 sqm/hr 3 hr battery H13 HEPA Elevator integrated InterContinental Wien reference 91 kg weight $42K / $881mo

Pros

  • Highest suction power in comparison (24 kPa) — deep carpet capability
  • Proven hotel deployments: InterContinental Wien, 50+ Hanting Hotels
  • Elevator integration via API/IoT — autonomous multi-floor
  • 3D camera auto-detects floor type and switches mode
  • Three carpet modes (light/medium/deep) for different pile heights
  • H13 medical-grade HEPA filtration
  • Diffuser variant adds ambient scenting — luxury differentiation
  • FieldBots OS for multi-vendor fleet management

Cons

  • Very heavy at 91 kg — difficult to reposition manually
  • Large footprint (80x69x89 cm) — may struggle in tight spaces
  • Premium price: $42,000 / $881/mo RaaS
  • Noise level not published (concerning for guest floors)
  • Practical coverage (400-800 sqm/hr) below C30's low end
  • Newer to US market; service network unknown
Verdict: Best raw cleaning performance and proven hotel track record, but expensive and heavy. If budget is not the primary constraint and InterContinental-level references are valued, this is the top pick.
3

SoftBank Robotics Whiz

SoftBank Robotics · Powered by BrainOS · Purpose-Built Carpet Vacuum

Safest Contingency
~2-3 kPa suction 465-557 sqm/hr 3 hr hot-swap 62 dB HEPA 30K+ BrainOS units global $25-42K / $1,042mo

Pros

  • Most established commercial carpet vacuum in Western market
  • 30,000+ BrainOS robots deployed globally — mature, proven
  • 62 dB — quiet enough for guest floors
  • Hot-swappable batteries enable true 24/7 operation
  • HEPA filtration with 4L dust bags
  • Memorizes up to 600 cleaning routes
  • SoftBank Connect provides real-time analytics + proof-of-clean
  • Elevator integration via RobotLAB Smart Pass
  • Light at 30 kg — staff-friendly
  • Wide US dealer network (Canon, RobotLAB, NISSCO)

Cons

  • Single-purpose vacuum only — no scrubbing/mopping
  • Suction not published; likely lower than Chinese competitors
  • Price variance: $25K-$42K depending on dealer
  • 4-hour charge time is longest in comparison
  • Dated design — hardware not significantly updated
  • Adds another vendor to the fleet (not PUDU)
Verdict: Safest, most proven choice with best US support infrastructure. Ideal if minimizing deployment risk is the priority. However, single-purpose device at a premium price, and hardware is aging.

Also Evaluated — Not Recommended

Karcher KIRA CV 50 · LionsBot R3 Vac · Tailos Rosie 2.0

Karcher KIRA CV 50

No Elevator Integration

Best-in-class 57 dB noise and IEC 63327 public-use safety certification. Lightest at 15 kg. $12,999 — most competitive price. However, no elevator integration is a dealbreaker for 7-floor deployment, narrow 35 cm cleaning width, 140-min battery, basic app.

Verdict: Excellent for single-floor or noise-critical deployments. Could complement the primary robot as supplementary unit for sensitive areas.

LionsBot R3 Vac

Partial Elevator Only

Red Dot Award winner. AI SmartClean with MagicTag zero-click maps. 61.7 dB, 6 L HEPA tank, LionsOS dashboard. However, elevator is "meet at lift" (not fully autonomous), 53 kg, $22,275 purchase is expensive for vacuum-only. R5 coming may obsolete R3.

Verdict: Solid mid-range, but lift coordination requires staff involvement — defeats autonomous purpose.

Tailos Rosie 2.0

Not Corridor-Grade

Miami-based manufacturer — local support advantage. Ultra-light (8 kg), $479/mo. But effectively consumer-grade: 28 cm width, 185 sqm/hr (well below commercial), 1.7L dustbin, 90-180 min battery, no elevator integration, no enterprise fleet management.

Verdict: Better for in-room housekeeping productivity than corridor cleaning. Could complement a corridor robot for room turns.

Final Recommendation

1 Primary Recommendation

Switch C30 → PUDU CC1 Pro

Consolidate the Thesis Hotel cleaning fleet on PUDU. The MT1 Max (outdoor courtyard sweeper) is already in Option B/C, and the CC1 Pro for carpet corridors means one vendor, one fleet management platform, one elevator integration standard, and one support relationship.

Versatility
4-in-1 platform — vacuum carpets AND scrub lobby hard floors
Performance
17,000 Pa · 700-1,000 sqm/hr · 4 hr carpet battery
Pricing
$22K purchase · $479-917/mo RaaS
Deployment Approach

Lease one CC1 Pro unit. Map guest floors 4-10 plus lobby. Schedule overnight carpet vacuuming (11 PM - 5 AM) with autonomous elevator transit between floors. Use scrub mode for lobby and hard-floor areas during low-traffic periods.

2 Secondary

Gausium Vacuum 40

If budget allows and raw cleaning is the priority. 24 kPa and InterContinental Wien reference are compelling. Diffuser variant adds unique luxury touch. At $42K / 91 kg, it's a larger commitment.

3 Contingency

SoftBank Whiz

If minimizing deployment risk is paramount. Largest installed base, most mature US support. Fall back here if PUDU elevator integration proves difficult at Thesis.

4 Monitor

ECOVACS K1 VAC

Request demo + US pricing. If ECOVACS establishes reliable US commercial sales and support in 2026, K1 VAC could become top recommendation — specs and built-in elevator are excellent on paper.

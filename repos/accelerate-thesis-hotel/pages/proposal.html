In-Room Dining Workflow

The S100 is a transport platform — it moves the meal between two humans. The choreography is designed so your F&B standards and health-code compliance are never compromised by the robot.

1

Guest orders. In-room tablet, phone, or property app → existing POS (Simphony, Toast).

2

Kitchen plates. Your runner plates on porcelain at the pass — same as today.

3

Load thermal modules. Hot tier: stainless cloche (140°F+). Cold: gel-pack cradle (40°F−). Runner taps "dispatch."

4

Dispatch. S100 rides elevator to room. 4–7 min pass → door. Temp logged per order.

5

Guest notified. Call to room PBX + knock-tone. Guest taps screen to unlock (key tap or room-number confirm).

6

Tray removal. Top tier slides out as a single tray. Robot waits 90 sec for additional tiers.

7

Pickup return. Scheduled sweep (every 45 min) or door-hanger-triggered. Runner reloads for dish-room.

What This Preserves

  • ✓ Staff plates, loads, retrieves. Robot replaces the elevator-and-corridor walk — the lowest-value leg of the trip.
  • ✓ Food-safety compliance. Temperature logged per order; thermal modules NSF-certified; phase-change packs cycle through walk-in on banquet-caddy schedule.
  • ✓ Plating standards. Top tier is a removable tray the guest sees open — looks like room-service plating, not "robot output." Walnut/brass shell is cosmetic; thermal modules work invisibly.

What This Does NOT Claim

  • ✕ Not a replacement for in-room dining attendants on premium orders (suite service, Champagne setups, table-side).
  • ✕ Not a carrier for items requiring active heating — no ovens on board.
  • ✕ Not a substitute for F&B runners during 7–10 AM rush. Humans handle complex orders; S100 handles simple parallel runs (continental, coffee, amenity drops).

Elevator Operations

Thesis operates 2 ThyssenKrupp TAC32T elevators serving guest floors 4–10. Robot-elevator coexistence is a design constraint we've built the operating plan around.

Guest-Yield Protocol

Robots always yield to waiting guests. If a guest is waiting on any floor, the robot holds position and takes the next available car. Guests never wait for a robot.

Off-Peak Scheduling

Cleaning robots operate 11 PM–6 AM when elevator demand is minimal. Full floor sequences before returning to dock — minimizing total trips.

Batch Transport

Linen and supply robots batch their loads — one full cart per trip instead of multiple partial runs. Fewer elevator trips, more efficient logistics.

Non-Invasive Integration

Our universal button emulator doesn't modify ThyssenKrupp firmware or require an OEM API contract. Fully reversible hardware layer.

Realistic impact at peak fleet (Option C): 8–12 additional elevator trips/hour during overnight operations; 3–5 trips/hour during daytime (on-demand deliveries only). Two elevators serving 7 guest floors handle ~40–60 trips/hour at capacity. Robot traffic is a modest incremental load concentrated in off-peak windows.

Facility Intelligence Platform — What You're Actually Buying

Option C includes a facility-wide sensing and dashboard layer. The structure is deliberate: Thesis owns the physical infrastructure; Accelerate licenses the software that runs on top of it.

The Physical Infrastructure

BLE Ceiling Anchor

77 UNITS

Ceiling-mounted, battery-powered. Room-level location for any asset within ~5m.

UWB Precision Anchor

11 UNITS

Wall/corner mount at key chokepoints (elevators, back-of-house). Sub-30cm accuracy.

BLE Asset Tag

102 UNITS

Adhesive or clip-on. Attached to carts, laundry bins, maintenance kits, linen totes.

How It Works

1

Tag Attached

An asset tag is affixed to a service cart, laundry bin, or maintenance kit. Each tag broadcasts a unique ID every few seconds.

2

Anchors Listen

Ceiling + wall anchors pick up the signal. Three or more anchors triangulate the tag's position in real time.

3

Dashboard Updates

Position data streams to the facility dashboard. Staff see every tagged asset on a live floor map — where it is, how long it's been idle.

4

Robot Dispatched

Rules trigger action — a cart idle >20 min in a corridor? The nearest robot is dispatched to retrieve it. No ticket, no radio call.

Same infrastructure; different use cases. Once the anchors are live, new workflows are software configuration — not new hardware.

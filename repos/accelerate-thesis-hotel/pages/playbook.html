Network Setup

WiFi is the lifeline — get this right first

⚠ 2.4GHz Only
All Keenon robots only support 2.4GHz WiFi (802.11b/g/n). They cannot connect to 5GHz networks. Ensure the facility has a dedicated 2.4GHz SSID.

Hotel/Hospital WiFi Tip
Request a dedicated SSID on the operations/staff network — not the guest WiFi. Guest networks often have captive portals, bandwidth throttling, and client isolation that will break robot connectivity.

Unboxing & Physical Setup

Handle with care — these are precision instruments

Two-person lift required. W3 is 48kg, C30 and T8 are 35kg each. Always keep robots upright during unboxing. Never lift by the head/screen, trays, or LiDAR housing.

Step 1 — Unpack

  • Remove packing belt, then outer box from bottom to top
  • Remove filling/foam material
  • Keep all packaging for potential returns/transport

Step 2 — Inspect

  • Check for shipping damage — dents, scratches, cracked screens
  • Verify all package contents against inventory checklist (Section 3)
  • Ensure battery is installed (C30 battery is removable)

Step 3 — Position

  • Move robot to its designated operating area
  • W3: Lift by force-bearing areas on both sides of body (middle position)
  • T8: Hold upper tray and lift from bottom force-bearing point
  • C30: Use handle when raised to guide robot
  • Clear the immediate area of cables, debris, and sharp objects

Step 4 — Attach Components

  • C30: Install side brushes (×2), sweeping brush, dust bag, and dust push board
  • W3: No assembly required — compartments are pre-installed
  • T8: Verify all 3 trays are securely attached

Charging Pile Installation

The robot's home base — placement is critical

Placement Requirements

  • Against a wall with clear approach path (2m minimum clear zone in front)
  • Within 1m of a 100-240V power outlet
  • Low-traffic area — robot will auto-dock frequently
  • Flat, level floor surface
  • Away from direct sunlight and heat sources
  • Easily accessible for maintenance
  • Not near stairs, glass walls, or reflective surfaces

Power On & Initial Boot

First boot takes ~40 seconds — be patient

First Boot Sequence
Power on → Boot logo → PEANUT animation (W3/T8) → Android desktop → PEANUT APP auto-launches → Ready (~40 seconds total)
  1. Ensure battery is installed and E-stop is not engaged
  2. Locate the power switch on the robot body
  3. Press power switch — blue light strip in the LiDAR gap will illuminate
  4. Wait ~40 seconds for system startup
  5. The 7" expression screen will display — robot is ready
  6. Select cleaning mode: Vacuuming (vacuum + sweep) or Dust-pushing
  1. Locate the power switch between the chassis and main body (left side when facing the robot)
  2. Press power switch — blue light strip illuminates
  3. Wait ~40 seconds: boot logo → PEANUT animation → Android desktop
  4. PEANUT APP launches automatically on the 11.6" touch screen
  5. If PEANUT APP doesn't launch, tap its icon on the Android desktop
  6. Tap the three-line menu (top-left) → enter PIN 0000 → Settings
  1. Open the rubber cover on the back of the robot (right side)
  2. Press the main power switch once
  3. Wait ~40 seconds for full boot
  4. Operation screen on top will display the home interface
  5. The stop button is under the silicone cover on the chassis — red button on right
  6. Verify tray indicator lights are working
First charge recommended. Fully charge the robot before first use, especially if the battery is below 20%. T8 takes ~5h, W3 takes ~6.5h, C30 takes 2-3h.

Facility Mapping

The robot learns its world — must be done by Keenon technician

Mapping requires a Keenon-certified technician. This is not a user-serviceable step. Schedule the technician visit in advance. Remote mapping may be available — confirm with Keenon support.

Pre-Mapping Preparation

During Mapping

The technician will walk the robot through all operating areas. The robot uses LiDAR and vision sensors to build a centimeter-accurate map.

Mapping typically takes 1-3 hours depending on facility size.

The technician will configure:

  • Delivery points (room numbers, table numbers)
  • Charging pile location
  • Standby/waiting points
  • No-go zones and virtual walls
  • Navigation routes and preferred paths
Re-mapping triggers: Remodeling/renovation, major furniture rearrangement, robot moved to new area, charging pile relocated. Contact Keenon support to schedule.

Delivery Mode Setup

Configure how each robot delivers

W3 ButlerBot — Delivery Workflow

  1. Tap Delivery icon on PEANUT APP
  2. Enter password → destination selection page
  3. Select compartment → door opens automatically
  4. Enter mobile phone number or floor/room number of destination
  5. Load items into compartment
  6. Tap Close Door → robot navigates to destination
  7. Robot notifies recipient (voice/call/SMS based on settings)
  8. Recipient taps Close Door after pickup → robot returns to base

If door isn't closed manually, it auto-closes on timeout.

T8 Dinnerbot — Five Delivery Modes

1

Delivery Mode

Multi-table food delivery. One tray per table. Auto-routes for fastest delivery. Tap tray icon → assign table number → tap Go.

2

Snack Delivery Mode

Cruises a preset route offering samples/beverages. Pauses when someone approaches or touches screen. Select route (1-6) → tap Go.

3

Multi-Destination Mode

Delivers to up to 20 tables in one trip. Like Snack mode but with specific table targets.

4

Direct Delivery Mode

Goes to one table, waits for next task (no auto-return). Best for takeout counters and pickup stations.

5

Birthday Mode

Navigates to destination while playing celebration songs. Playlist configurable.

Elevator Integration

W3 only — enables multi-floor delivery

Full elevator installation guide: Thesis Hotel Elevator Integration Guide →

E-Box System Overview

  • E-Box Master: Fixed on top of elevator shaft — requires 220V power + Ethernet (RJ45)
  • E-Box Slave: Fixed on top of elevator cabin — requires 220V power
  • RFID Tags: Fixed on shaft wall at each floor level
  • RFID Reader: Fixed on top of cabin — reads tags as cabin passes each floor
  • LoRa Antennas: On Master and Slave — must be placed parallel in shaft

Communication Methods

  • Direct (LoRa): Robot → E-Box Slave → cabin panel IO
  • Cloud-routed: Robot → WiFi → Cloud → E-Box Master → LoRa → E-Box Slave → cabin panel

Max 28 floors supported per E-Box (32 IO channels minus door open + reserved)

Staff Training

Your deployment is only as good as the team operating it

C30 — Cleaning Staff

  • Start/stop/pause cleaning
  • Switch between vacuum and dust-push modes
  • Empty dust bag and clean filters
  • Replace side brushes and sweeping brush
  • Send to charging pile
  • What to do if robot gets stuck
  • Emergency stop location and operation

W3 — Front Desk / Concierge

  • Load items into compartments
  • Enter room numbers and dispatch
  • Handle delivery failures / stuck robot
  • Modify or cancel in-progress tasks
  • Manual charging procedure
  • Emergency stop location (back of robot)
  • Guest interaction: what to tell guests about the robot

T8 — Kitchen / Service Staff

  • Load trays correctly (5kg top, 10kg bottom)
  • Assign table numbers per tray
  • Select correct delivery mode
  • Pause/resume mid-delivery
  • Handle spills (wipe immediately, robot is NOT waterproof)
  • Emergency stop (top of robot)
  • Never remove items from moving robot
T8 Safety: The T8 manual explicitly states it should not be used near electronic medical equipment or in health care facilities. If deploying in a hospital, use the W3 for clinical areas instead.

Troubleshooting

Common issues and how to fix them

ProblemProbable CauseSolution
Robot won't power on Low/dead battery Charge with power adapter (not charging pile). If persists, contact Keenon support.
Robot won't charge Charging pile moved, no power, or blocked Verify pile is in original mapped location, connected to power, and path is clear.
Navigation fails Positioning error, dirty sensors, or LiDAR blocked 1) Move robot to charging pile to restore position. 2) Clean LiDAR and vision modules. 3) Check if collision switch is stuck.
Robot doesn't move E-stop engaged or obstacle detected 1) Check and release e-stop. 2) Move label/robot to visible area. 3) If persists, power cycle.
No voice/sound Volume off or too low Adjust volume in PEANUT APP settings.
WiFi disconnected Signal lost or network changed Check 2.4GHz SSID is broadcasting. Re-enter WiFi credentials on robot. Verify no captive portal.
C30 poor cleaning Full dust bag, worn brushes, or wrong mode Empty dust bag, replace brushes, verify correct mode for surface type.
T8 delivery to wrong table Table numbers remapped or mislabeled Verify table number mapping in system. Re-mapping may be needed if tables moved.
Robot falls down Unexpected step, slope, or obstacle Power off immediately. Contact Keenon support. Do not attempt to restart.

Emergency Procedures

Every staff member must know these

⚠ Critical Safety Rule
Never override or disable the emergency stop. If a robot behaves unexpectedly, press E-stop first, diagnose second.

Emergency Stop Locations

C30

Location: Emergency stop button on the rear body panel

Action: Press button to stop immediately. Release to resume.

W3

Location: Emergency stop switch on the back of the robot (behind cover)

Action: Press to stop. Robot disconnects from power immediately.

T8

Location: Emergency stop switch on top of the robot

Action: Press to stop. Rotate switch in indicated direction to resume.

Slope warning: If the emergency stop is pressed while the robot is on a slope, the robot may continue moving due to inertia. Ensure personnel safety first.

11. Observations for Robot Deployment

Facts from the profile that directly shape how we plan robot work at this site:

  • Rooftop pool is on L3, not ground level. The pool-deck slip-hazard use case runs on a 3rd-floor rooftop reached via hotel lobby elevators. Any pool-deck robot must fit in a guest elevator for initial placement. Deck material still unconfirmed (Kool-Deck vs. travertine vs. pavers).
  • Paseo Courtyard is long and narrow (25 × 202 ft). Earlier site estimates of 800–1,500 m² were too generous. Working figure is ~5,050 sq ft cocktail area, with a ~65 × 33 ft padel court eating ~32% of the length.
  • Padel is confirmed in the courtyard — not pickleball, not tennis. Dynamic obstacle during play. Robot routing must route around the court footprint or execute during non-play windows.
  • Courtyard is semi-public. The "Urban Living Room" framing means robots will mix with residents, UM students, neighbors, yoga classes (Thu/Sat mornings), farmers markets, padel players, wedding setups, and live-music events. The opposite of a controlled environment.
  • 10-story hotel wing with guest rooms on floors 4–10. Any room-service or delivery robot traverses the full vertical stack via ThyssenKrupp TAC32T elevators.
  • Inferred autonomous window: ~11pm–6am. Pool closes 6:30pm; Ivy Rooftop kitchen 11pm; Collab/Mamey dinner 10–11pm; Mamey breakfast 6:30am; pool opens 7am.
  • Event surges — Paseo hosts 500-person cocktails and 200-seat dinners; Theory Ballroom up to 250 reception. Routing should adapt to setup/breakdown schedules.
  • Back-of-house / loading dock: unknown. Must be confirmed on site walk.
  • Curio Collection under Hilton means the hotel participates in LightStay / EarthView. Brand-level integrations (loyalty, Honors app, centralized ops standards) may bias against standalone robot tech unless Hilton-approved or explicitly carved out.
  • Meeting room AV standard (Meeting Owl Pro, built-in share-screen in every room) signals a tech-friendly operations culture.
  • $30.51 destination fee already bundles wellness perks — a precedent for bundling "robot-enabled service" into a single guest line item (useful framing for RaaS commercial structure).
  • Two-building mixed use means hotel robots should stay on the hotel side. Residences are a separate Gables Residential property.

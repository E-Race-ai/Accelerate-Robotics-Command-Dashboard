§ A

Who this is for

This package is written to be read end-to-end by the integration lead, but each discipline can jump to the sections that matter most.

Industrial Designer
What changes, what stays fixed
The dock zone (§3) is an inviolable keep-out region on the underside. Everything above the dock plane is yours — chassis styling, trim kits, materials, finishes. The Moore / Thesis / Service trim matrix rides on the common chassis. Read: §1, §2 reference carts, §5 open items.
Mechanical Engineer
Dimensions, loads, tolerances
Start at §1 (Dock Standard v1) — eight normative requirements with rationale. Then §3 (Lift Kits) for the robot-side mechanism details: cam actuation, 8 mm travel, passive detent. §4 gives you the host-robot envelopes the kits must fit within.
Fabricator
Materials, hardware, finishes
§2 cart specs call out castor size, tread, brake type, powder-coat colors, leather/veneer callouts per trim kit. §1.3.3 defines the lift-pad hardware (30×30 mm hardened steel, flush mount, rigid-frame attachment).
Robot OEM
What we need from you
We're seeking accessory-platform bolt-hole drawings, power/data connector pinouts, and a policy statement on third-party mechanical accessories. See §4 for the host-robot reference data we already have, §6 for the formal asks. Pudu T300 integration is gated on spec acquisition.
§ 1

Dock Standard v1 — Normative Requirements

Engineer
Fabricator
Robot OEM

A cart is v1-compliant if and only if it satisfies all eight of the following. No partial compliance. The full spec including glossary, scope, and versioning policy is at docs/standard/dock-standard-v1.md.

Dock zone (§3.1 keep-out)
Lift pads (§3.3 four corners)
Pad-pattern rectangle (750 × 400)
Fiducial (§3.5 AprilTag 36h11)
§3.1 · DOCK FOOTPRINT
Clear rectangle on cart underside
900 mm (L) × 550 mm (W) — long axis ∥ travel
Sized to accommodate the Keenon S300 base (700×900 mm) with approach margin. Smaller robots enter with clearance to spare.
§3.2 · OVERHEAD CLEARANCE
Floor-to-cart vertical gap within zone
≥ 260 mm
S300 max height ≈ 240 mm including top sensors; 20 mm margin absorbs floor unevenness and lift-kit stack-up.
§3.3 · LIFT-PAD PATTERN
Four steel pads on a 750×400 rectangle
750 mm × 400 mm · pads 30 × 30 mm · hardened steel · flush
Four pads distribute mass evenly. Rigid-frame attachment required — not to trim, panels, or shrouds.
§3.4 · LIFT TRAVEL
Vertical displacement on engagement
8 mm ± 1 mm · ≤ 1° tilt off horizontal
Clears castor compression (3–4 mm under load) plus safety margin. Tight enough to preserve CG stability at 350 kg.
§3.5 · FIDUCIAL
AprilTag alignment target
36h11 family · 80 × 80 mm · matte · 200 mm from leading edge, center-lateral
36h11 tolerates partial occlusion. Tag IDs reserved by property — request allocation before manufacture.
§3.6 · ALIGNMENT TOLERANCE
Accepted pose error at engagement
Linear ± 15 mm (X, Y) · Angular ± 3° yaw
Achievable by Keenon-class robot in corridor conditions. Tighter → high abort rate; looser → tip-over risk.
§3.7 · MAX GROSS MASS
Cart + payload combined
≤ 350 kg
Covers S300's 300 kg payload + ~50 kg cart tare. Smaller carts self-limit (C1 = 95 kg gross max).
§3.8 · ELECTRICAL INTERFACE
Passive cart — no power, no wiring
Fully passive · BLE stick-on tag optional (not integral)
Eliminates charging dependencies and electrical failure modes. v2 reserved for inductive charging.
Validation Tool Automated dimensional check against all §3 requirements: pages/compliance-check.html. A physical compliance jig (steel frame) is a Phase 1 deliverable.

Engagement sequence (common)

1Dispatch — robot receives fetch-cart task with target AprilTag ID
2Approach — navigate to corridor (BLE tag narrows to ~1 m if deployed)
3Visual acquisition — alignment camera resolves tag, estimates relative pose
4Approach refinement — close to within §3.6 tolerance (±15 mm, ±3°)
5Drive-under — enter dock zone at < 0.2 m/s, proximity sensors monitoring
6Lift engage — solenoid fires, cam rotates, pads rise 8 mm, detent captures
7Transit — normal speed, passive detent (no ongoing power)
8Drop-off — reverse solenoid, cam rotates, pads drop, cart rests on castors
9Egress — robot backs out at < 0.2 m/s, cart stationary
S300-LK Binding Constraints The S300 base is 240 mm tall. With the §3.2 minimum clearance at 260 mm, the S300-LK has only 20 mm of height budget. This drives the low-profile cam design. Also verify LiDAR/top sensors do not intrude above the platform — full dimensional validation required before kit freeze.

§ 6

Scope, Versioning & Open Items

In scope (v1)

  • Cart underside geometry (dock zone)
  • Lift-pad pattern and load-transfer contact points
  • Vision fiducial for robot alignment
  • Cart mass limits
  • Clearance requirements

Out of scope (v1)

  • Cart upper structure (above the dock plane)
  • Cart aesthetic / finish / materials (trim-kit decisions)
  • Robot navigation, mapping, fleet orchestration
  • Electrical / inductive charging — reserved for v2
  • Outdoor or rough-surface use

Versioning policy

v1.x — additive-only changes (rationale, informative notes, tooling). No normative parameter changes. v1-compliant carts and lift kits are forward-compatible with any v1.x revision.

v2 — reserved for future evolution. Candidate additions: inductive charging contacts, NFC identity chip for cart-specific routing, heavier gross-mass tier.

Formal asks (by audience)

  • Robot OEMKeenon bolt-hole CAD (S100 + S300)

  • FabricatorCastor-supplier validation (auto-brake 5 s behavior)
  • EngineerDimensional validation against physical S300 unit
  • EngineerAprilTag ID allocation scheme across properties
  • EngineerPhysical compliance jig fabrication

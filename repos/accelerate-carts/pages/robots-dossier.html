Dock-kit integration analysis

Fits the Dock Standard v1 envelope

  • Base footprint: ~550 × 700 mm chassis (tall mast brings overall H to 1282 mm) — fits comfortably under the 900 × 550 mm dock zone with ~200 mm forward/aft margin.
  • Base height: ~200 mm chassis height. With the 60 mm S100-LK stack-up, combined height is right at the 260 mm §3.2 minimum. Validate before kit freeze.
  • Payload: 100 kg rating limits the S100 to lighter v1-compliant carts (C1 food @ 95 kg gross, H1 housekeeping @ 110 kg gross — borderline, size-load carefully).
  • Accessory mount: Flat platform with bolt-hole pattern + low-voltage connector — suitable for the S100-LK mechanical mount. Exact pattern must be requested from Keenon.

Known gaps (need from Keenon)

  • Threshold / groove crossing — not published. Affects corridor-sill + elevator-transition viability.
  • Gradient / slope rating — not published. Internal estimate is ~5° from the Keenon family default.
  • Accessory-plate bolt-hole CAD — gating S100-LK mechanical mount design.
  • Accessory-connector pinout — gates any v2 electrical integration.
  • IP rating / environmental — affects outdoor-covered walkway use cases.
  • SDK / fleet API — gates software integration path beyond PEANUT APP.

Dock-kit integration analysis

Binds two Dock Standard parameters

  • §3.1 dock footprint: the 900 × 550 mm dock zone was sized around the S300's ~700 × 900 mm base footprint — 200 mm of approach margin on the long axis.
  • §3.2 clearance: the S300's base is ~240 mm tall; with the 20 mm low-profile S300-LK on top, combined height sits exactly at the 260 mm minimum. Zero margin. This drives the kit's cam-mechanism profile.
  • §3.7 gross mass: 300 kg payload + ~50 kg cart tare = 350 kg cap. If the S300 payload rating is confirmed conservative, v2 could lift the cap.
  • Platform: 860 × 600 mm native — fits the 750 × 400 pad pattern with 55 mm margin around it.

Known gaps (need from Keenon)

  • Max moving speed — critical for drive-under sequencing; need documented value.
  • Slope rating — informs 3° yaw + grade tipping validation with 300 kg payload.
  • Accessory bolt-hole CAD + connector pinout (shared ask with S100).
  • Sensor tower clearance from accessory-platform top — must not intrude above 260 mm.
  • LiDAR mounting geometry — if top-LiDAR shadow crosses the dock zone plane, cart mass shifts may disrupt localization.
S300-LK binding constraint The 20 mm kit-height budget on top of the 240 mm base drives a low-profile cam design distinct from the S100-LK's 60 mm allowance. The S300-LK reuses ~85 % of S100-LK subcomponents but requires different frame/cam parts. Manufacturing partner selection blocks on this.

AprilTag detection — common configuration

AprilTag 36h11 reader spec — shared across S100-LK, S300-LK, T300-LK

  • Tag family: 36h11 (strong error correction; matches Keenon default so PEANUT APP extension is feasible without porting)
  • Tag size: 80 mm × 80 mm white border + black-on-white interior
  • Mount: face-down on cart underside, 200 mm from leading edge, centered laterally
  • Camera: USB 720p @ 30 fps min, ≥ 120° horizontal FoV
  • Camera height: 50 mm above robot base, angled 10° downward
  • Read distance: 0.5–1.5 m typical approach
  • Detection rate: 10 Hz minimum; publishes relative pose (x, y, yaw) to lift controller
  • Alignment pose published to: robot navigation stack for final approach refinement within § 3.6 tolerance (±15 mm / ±3°)
  • Tag ID scheme: one tag ID per cart, allocated per property by Accelerate Robotics (contact before cart manufacture)

Fleet-orchestration integration path

Per-robot orchestration — what we do while OEM SDKs remain closed

  • Keenon (S100/S300): Phase 1 uses PEANUT APP extensions + Keenon Cloud scraping. Phase 2 attempts direct PEANUT APP integration (requires Keenon support letter). Long-term: Keenon OEM partnership for a proper SDK.
  • Pudu (T300): Phase 3 onset uses the Open Platform endpoint via documented ask. Same escalation path if docs are withheld.
  • Our fleet manager: BLE asset-tag positioning narrows cart location to a corridor; dispatch sends robot to corridor; robot's onboard AprilTag detector handles final approach. This keeps the fleet-layer robot-agnostic.
  • Elevator integration: Keenon E-Box is the known working path (see /Users/ericrace/Code/accelerate-elevator/ repo). Pudu equivalent requires vendor ask.

Pre-deployment site-readiness checklist

Run this before committing a robot to any site. Open items → fix the site, reduce scope, or defer.

  • Corridor width survey — measure every corridor on the robot's route. Document min passage width vs robot's required. Flag anything tighter than spec. Tool: tape measure. Deliverable: floor plan with corridor widths annotated.
  • Threshold audit — photograph every doorway and floor transition on route. Measure vertical step. Anything > 20 mm is a ramp-accommodation or route-change decision. Tool: feeler gauge / ruler + phone camera.
  • Elevator route — if multi-floor, confirm E-Box (Keenon) or Pudu-equivalent installation feasibility with the building's elevator contractor. See accelerate-elevator/.
  • WiFi site survey — walk the full robot route with a WiFi analyzer. Confirm 2.4 GHz signal ≥ –65 dBm at every waypoint. Flag dead zones. Tool: any WiFi analyzer app or Ekahau.
  • Network segment & SSID — dedicated robot SSID (separate from guest) with VLAN isolation. Document SSID + pre-shared key for commissioning.
  • Charging-dock placement — identify a 1.2 × 0.8 m back-of-house floor location with 110 V/220 V AC, near the main route, < 20 m from primary work zone.
  • Floor material & condition — hard or hotel-grade carpet (no plush carpet > 10 mm pile). Photo document any weak spots.
  • Ambient lighting — confirm corridors are lit at night operating hours. Robot vision degrades below 50 lux. Pair with facility maintenance to raise corridor lumens if needed.
  • Ambient temperature — confirm 0–40 °C year-round in service zones (robot will not ship if cold storage or freezer zone).
  • Staff training plan — minimum 2 hours hands-on per shift (PEANUT APP PIN + emergency release + manual push-off). Schedule with ops lead.
  • Cart-dock placement — identify drop-off + pickup zones in the hotel program. Ensure each has a 1 m × 1 m clear radius for the cart.
  • Insurance / liability review — facility wants ISO 3691-4 certification on file (T300 has it; Keenon pair does not publish). Coordinate with legal.
§ 10

Dock Standard v1 — full normative text

Verbatim reproduction of docs/standard/dock-standard-v1.md. This is the contract all three lift kits are designed against. A cart is v1-compliant if and only if it satisfies all of § 3.1 through § 3.8.

THE DOCK ZONE · TOP VIEW · 900 × 550 mm

§ 3.1 Dock footprint

The cart underside must contain a clear rectangular region of 900 mm (length) × 550 mm (width), oriented with the long axis parallel to the cart's normal travel direction. This region is the "dock zone."

Rationale: 900 × 550 mm accommodates the Keenon S300 base with margin for approach tolerance. Smaller robots enter the same zone with clearance to spare.

§ 3.2 Overhead clearance

The dock zone must have ≥ 260 mm of vertical clearance measured from the floor to the lowest-hanging cart component within the zone.

Rationale: Keenon S300 maximum base height is ~ 240 mm including top sensors. 20 mm margin prevents contact during approach.

§ 3.3 Lift-pad pattern

The cart must present four lift pads within the dock zone, arranged as the corners of a rectangle:

  • Rectangle dimensions: 750 mm × 400 mm
  • Rectangle center: coincident with the dock zone center
  • Pad dimensions: 30 mm × 30 mm each
  • Pad material: steel, hardened, flush with the cart underside (no protrusion)

Pads must be rigidly affixed to the cart's load-bearing frame — not to finish panels, shrouds, or trim.

§ 3.4 Lift travel

When engaged, the robot lifts the cart 8 mm ± 1 mm above its resting height. All four pads must support the cart at equivalent height — the cart must not rock or tilt more than 1° off horizontal during lift.

Rationale: 8 mm clears typical castor compression (3–4 mm under load) plus safety margin, and preserves CG stability under 350 kg gross.

§ 3.5 Fiducial (alignment target)

The cart must present a single AprilTag fiducial on a face-down surface within the dock zone:

  • Tag family: 36h11 (matches Keenon default)
  • Tag size: 80 mm × 80 mm (white-bordered, black-on-white interior)
  • Tag position: geometric center 200 mm from the leading edge, centered laterally
  • Tag surface: matte
  • Tag IDs: reserved by property — ask Accelerate for allocation before manufacturing

§ 3.6 Alignment tolerance

At engagement, the robot and cart must be aligned within:

  • Linear: ± 15 mm in both X (forward) and Y (lateral)
  • Angular: ± 3° in yaw (rotation about vertical axis)

Beyond these tolerances, the robot MUST abort the lift attempt, reposition, and re-approach.

§ 3.7 Maximum cart mass (gross)

The combined mass of cart + payload must not exceed 350 kg.

Rationale: Covers S300's 300 kg payload rating plus ~50 kg cart tare.

§ 3.8 Electrical interface

In v1, the cart must be fully passive. No batteries, motors, wiring, or connectors within the dock zone or elsewhere on the cart (other than a stick-on BLE asset tag, which is an optional accessory).

Open items Dimensional validation against a physical Keenon S300 unit (§ 3.1, § 3.2) · AprilTag ID allocation scheme · physical compliance jig fabrication · v2 roadmap.

§ 12

Lift-kit reference designs

Mechanical + sensing architecture for the three robot-side kits. S100-LK is the reference; S300-LK is a scaled variant reusing ~85% of subcomponents; T300-LK is gated on Pudu CAD acquisition.

S100-LK — Architecture

Three functional elements, bolt-on accessory

  • Lift mechanism — cam-driven platform with four fixed 30 × 30 mm hardened-steel pads matching § 3.3. Spring-loaded retraction. Single 12 V solenoid trigger engages a passive mechanical detent that holds the lifted position without continuous current.
  • Alignment camera — USB wide-angle 720p @ 30 fps, ≥ 120° HFoV, mounted 50 mm above robot base at 10° downward tilt. AprilTag 36h11 detector runs on the S100's Android compute.
  • Proximity sensing — three HC-SR04 ultrasonic modules on front, left-side, right-side faces. Range 5–300 cm. Triggers lift abort if reading < 5 cm unexpectedly during drive-under.

S100-LK — Engagement workflow (9 steps)

  1. Dispatch. Robot receives a fetch-cart task with the target cart's AprilTag ID.
  2. Approach. Navigates to the corridor; BLE tag narrows to ~ 1 m.
  3. Visual acquisition. Alignment camera acquires the AprilTag; detector publishes relative X, Y, yaw.
  4. Approach refinement. Robot closes to within § 3.6 tolerance (± 15 mm / ± 3°).
  5. Drive-under. Enters dock zone at < 0.2 m/s. Proximity sensors monitor for unexpected contact.
  6. Lift engage. Solenoid fires; cam rotates; pads rise 8 mm; detent captures.
  7. Transit. Proceeds to destination at normal speed; detent requires no ongoing power.
  8. Drop-off. Release solenoid fires; pads drop 8 mm; cart rests on castors.
  9. Egress. Robot backs out at low speed; cart stationary.

S100-LK failure modes

  • AprilTag not acquired → "cart not found", returns to dock
  • Alignment tolerance exceeded → back off, re-approach; after 3 failures, report and return
  • Proximity sensor trips during drive-under → immediate halt, back off, re-attempt
  • Lift solenoid jams → manual release handle (hotel staff)
  • Power loss mid-transit → passive detent maintains hold; manual handle if needed

Day-1 engineering kickoff checklist

Everything an engineer needs to do on Monday morning to start the Accelerate Cart Platform project. Sequenced so that CAD work and vendor correspondence can run in parallel with hardware sourcing.

Read (2-3 hours)

  • Open this dossier top-to-bottom — §1 through §14. Pay special attention to §10 (Dock Standard v1 normative text) and §6 (dimensional overlay).
  • Read docs/superpowers/specs/2026-04-15-cart-platform-design.md end-to-end — the canonical design doc behind the standard.
  • Read docs/lift-kits/S100-LK.md and docs/lift-kits/S300-LK.md for the reference lift-kit architectures.
  • Read docs/carts/C1-food-cart.md, L1-linen-cart.md, H1-housekeeping.md — the three reference cart families.
  • Open assets/robots/pudu-t300/brochure.pdf, datasheet.pdf, and manual.pdf in a PDF reader. These are the only full-vendor-PDF robots we have — extract every dimension, load, and safety note.

CAD — start immediately (week 1)

  • Model the Dock Standard v1 keep-out volume in CAD: 900 mm × 550 mm × 260 mm rectangular envelope on cart underside. This is the authoritative reference geometry every downstream part derives from.
  • Model the four 750 × 400 mm lift-pad pattern as a separate part (studs, contact pad plates, anti-slip texture note).
  • Model the AprilTag 36h11 fiducial zone — 80 × 80 mm minimum, centered on the dock zone. Call out the ±15 mm / ±3° alignment tolerance as a GD&T note.
  • Build a simplified S100, S300, and T300 chassis stand-in (just the chassis below 260 mm) to dry-fit against the dock zone and verify clearance.
  • Push CAD to the shared Onshape / Fusion workspace and link it in docs/standard/dock-standard-v1.md as "reference CAD".

Vendor correspondence — week 1

  • Draft the Keenon vendor-ask letter — see §15.1. One-page request for S100 and S300 STEP files, lift-kit attachment point drawings, and motor-controller wiring diagrams. Send to Keenon US contact.
  • Draft the Pudu vendor-ask letter — see §15.2. Priority: get T300 STEP file, WTIDL1 lift SKU availability, and Pudu Open Platform SDK access. Send to Pudu US partner program.
  • Create a vendor-communication log at docs/reference/vendor-correspondence.md — every email, phone call, and meeting dated and summarized.

Hardware sourcing — week 1-2 (parallel)

  • Source three candidate heavy-duty auto-brake castors (150 kg each, ≥75 mm diameter, mechanical brake). Short list: Colson, Tente, Blickle. Order one set of each for side-by-side bench testing.
  • Source the AprilTag 36h11 fiducial printing stock — matte-laminated PET, UV-stable. Print 20 tags in the reference 80 mm size for all three robots.
  • Bench-test one 12 V / 3 A solenoid (S100-LK reference) and one 24 V / 5 A solenoid (S300-LK reference). Verify pull force, cycle life, dropout behavior.
  • Source aluminum extrusion stock (40 × 40 Bosch-style) for lift-kit frame prototyping.
  • Open purchase orders for: (a) one Keenon S100 dev unit, (b) one Pudu T300 dev unit. S300 can come later once the S300-LK design is validated.

Prototype — week 2-4

  • Build S100-LK v0 — 3D-printed cam mechanism + aluminum lift plate. Target: prove the 8 mm passive-lift geometry on the bench before cutting metal.
  • Write the AprilTag alignment test rig — a Python script using the apriltag library that reads a webcam feed and reports pose error in mm/degrees. Use this to characterize the ±15 mm / ±3° tolerance under realistic lighting.
  • Build C1-Generic — the first compliant food-cart chassis. Welded tube frame, four castors, reference lift-pad pattern, stick-on AprilTag. This is the first cart a robot will pick up.

Accelerate Carts
The Dock Standard

v1.0

A mechanical and sensing interface spec. Any cart meeting all eight requirements below is interoperable with any robot running a v1-compliant lift kit.

The Dock Zone — Top View

Dock zone 900×550 mm · lift pads 750×400 mm · fiducial 200 mm from leading edge

The Eight Normative Requirements

§3.1
Dock Footprint
Value
900 mm (length) × 550 mm (width)
Orientation
Long axis parallel to cart travel direction
Rationale. Accommodates the Keenon S300 base (~700×900 mm) with approach tolerance. Smaller robots enter with room to spare.
§3.2
Overhead Clearance
Minimum
260 mm from floor
Rationale. S300 base height (~240 mm) + 20 mm safety margin. Prevents drag on robot sensors.
§3.3
Lift-Pad Pattern
Count
4
Rectangle
750 × 400 mm, centered on dock zone
Pad size
30 × 30 mm, hardened steel
Mounting
Rigidly affixed to load-bearing frame (not trim)
Rationale. Four pads distribute mass evenly; 750×400 mm centers the pads within both the dock zone and the robot's lift platform.
§3.4
Lift Travel
Value
8 mm ± 1 mm
Tilt limit
≤ 1° off horizontal during lift
Rationale. Clears typical castor compression (3–4 mm) with safety margin. Preserves CG stability at 350 kg gross.
§3.5
Fiducial
Tag family
AprilTag 36h11
Size
80 × 80 mm
Position
200 mm from leading edge, laterally centered
Finish
Matte (no reflective coating)
ID allocation
Assigned per property by Accelerate
Rationale. 36h11 has strong error-correction for partial occlusion. 80 mm is readable at 0.5–1.5 m under standard corridor lighting.
§3.6
Alignment Tolerance
Linear
±15 mm in X (forward) and Y (lateral)
Angular
±3° in yaw
Beyond tolerance
Robot aborts, repositions, re-approaches
Rationale. Achievable by a Keenon-class robot in a typical corridor. Tighter tolerance creates high abort rates; looser risks pad misalignment.
§3.7
Maximum Gross Mass
Cart + payload
≤ 350 kg
Rationale. S300 payload rating (300 kg) + ~50 kg cart tare. Smaller carts self-limit per their chassis spec.
§3.8
Electrical Interface
v1 status
Fully passive — no batteries, motors, wiring, or connectors
Exceptions
Stick-on BLE asset tag is optional and not structurally integral
Rationale. Eliminates a whole class of failure modes. Carts never need charging; all motion is mechanical. Inductive charging reserved for v2.
Compliance is binary
A cart either meets all eight requirements or it is not v1-compliant. No partial compliance. Use the dimensional compliance checker to verify a design.

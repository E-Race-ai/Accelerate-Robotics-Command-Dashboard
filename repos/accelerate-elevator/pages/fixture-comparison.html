Accelerate Robotics Fixture Type Comparison

One Emulator,
Two Fixture Types

We've torn open elevator fixtures at two different buildings and found two completely different architectures. Here's why the same $20 board handles both.

Every elevator button does the same thing electrically: closing a contact between two conductors. Our SSR closes that same contact from a second path. The controller can't tell the difference.

Why One Board Handles Both

The ESP32-C3 + SSR architecture is universal. The SSR is just a switch — it doesn't know or care what's on the other side of the contact closure.

⚡

Same Electrical Principle

Whether it's shorting I/O#1 to G24 on a ThyssenKrupp PCB, or closing Wire A to Wire B on a discrete switch — it's just a contact closure. The controller sees a hall call.

📡

Same Firmware & Protocol

The BLE GATT protocol is identical. The robot sends "call elevator to floor 3" — the ESP32 fires the SSR for 500ms. Same code, same pairing, same watchdog.

🛡

Same Safety Guarantees

1500V galvanic isolation via optoisolated SSR. Hardware watchdog. Normally-open default. Power-loss-safe. No interference with fire recall or safety circuits.
